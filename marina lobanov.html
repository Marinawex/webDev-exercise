Favorite book

Humans: A Brief History of How We F*cked It All Up

The book gives examples of how human beings can do great and wonderful things and still mess things up spectacularly at the same time. He starts with Lucy, the Australopithecus skeleton found in the 1960s in Africa. Carrying on from there to describe how many of mankind's greatest achievements have also been in many unmitigated disasters. He also covers a large number of smaller more personal ones like the first recorded auto and train accident fatalities. The book which is quite dark by its very nature was written in such a way as to also be fairly humorous, although its a pretty dark humor. Along the way, I learned that the same man who invented leaded gasoline also invented CFCs and managed to strangle himself in another one of his own inventions later in his life.

A place I enjoyed visiting

Cinque Terre

The Cinque Terre, the five towns, is a string of five old fishing villages perched high on the Italian Riviera in the region Liguria, which until recently were linked only by mule tracks and accessible only by rail or water. Vernazza, Cinque TerreTo hike along this scenic coastline is the best way to explore the five villages: Monterosso, Vernazza, Corniglia, Manarola and Riomaggiore. The Cinque Terre is noted for its beauty. Over centuries, people have carefully built terraces to cultivate grapes and olives on the rugged, steep landscape right up to the cliffs that overlook the Mediterranean Sea. The breathtaking views of harbors far below the wild but hospitable coastline along with the medieval fortresses and plentiful vines and vibrant colours make this a memorable holiday.
